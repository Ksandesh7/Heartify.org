Heart Disease Exercise and Suggestions

Recommended Exercises

  • Cardiovascular exercises such as running, cycling, and swimming
  • Resistance training exercises such as weight lifting, push-ups, and squats
  • Flexibility exercises such as yoga and stretching

It is also important to maintain a regular exercise routine. Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise on most days of the week, or at least 150 minutes per week. Consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new exercise program, especially if you have been diagnosed with heart disease.

Lifestyle Suggestions

  • Eat a healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein sources such as fish and poultry
  • Avoid or limit processed and high-fat foods, sugary drinks, and excessive amounts of salt
  • Quit smoking and avoid exposure to secondhand smoke
  • Manage stress through techniques such as meditation, deep breathing, or yoga
  • Maintain a healthy weight and waist circumference
  • Monitor and manage high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and diabetes with the help of a healthcare professional
